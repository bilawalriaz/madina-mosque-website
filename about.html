Our History

Madina Mosque and Islamic Centre was established in 1992 in vacant shop premises as a teaching institute with the aim to serve, educate and inspire the local Muslim community.

Within two years we had outgrown those premises and needed a larger space. In 1994, with overwhelming support from our congregation, we purchased our current site, a former clothing factory for £65,000, and transformed it into a Madrasah.

In 1999 we were granted permission by the local authority to expand our services and became a Mosque. In 2002 we built a female-only prayer hall with a purpose-built library.

After years of fundraising, we broke ground in 2010 and completed our purpose-built Mosque in 2014. Since then further facelifts and improvements have been carried out.
